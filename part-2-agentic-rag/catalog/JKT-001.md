When the forecast turns against you, this is the layer that stands between
you and the weather. The Stormshield is a three-layer hardshell built for
sustained downpours and exposed alpine ridgelines — fully taped seams, a
storm-flapped front zip, and a hood that adjusts over a climbing helmet.

It is a shell, not an insulator: it shrugs off driving rain and wind but
adds no warmth on its own, so wear it over a fleece or a down mid-layer.
Pit zips dump heat on a hard climb. This is the jacket hikers trust when
the rain has set in for the whole day and turning back is not an option.
